Playoff Preview

Scampi (1) vs. Work Ass (8)

Some are calling this the de facto championship. The teams 1st and 3rd in points for face off in a colossal first round clash. Yahoo’s model has this as a 50-50 proposition. Our model favors Scampi a bit, but it remains far from a lock. X-factors: Jonathan Taylor and Rome Odunze. Taylor is the #1 running back in the league so how can he be an x-factor right? Well without Daniel Jones and Riley Leonard unavailable, Taylor might be running into a brick wall against Seattle’s defense. If that happens, Scampi’s recipe might be cooked. For Work Ass, Odunze has been scrambled ass for the past month. To advance to the semifinals, he’ll likely need Odunze to play like he did in September. Pick: Work Ass pulls the upset.

Ginny Sack (2) vs. Team OBAMA SOPRANOS (7)

Big Gin is a heavy favorite this week in a major contrast of roster construction. Obama is relying heavily on the Lamar-Burrow duo while Gin slapped together a Shedeur-Shough QB room to pair with a plethora of skill talent. Our model gives Gin a 73% chance to advance. Can Obama give one of his patented speeches to motivate the troops? X-factors: Kimani Vidal and Michael Wilson. Even with Omarion Hampton back in the lineup, Vidal saw a lot of touches on Monday night. Obama will need that to continue if he has any hope of generating enough RB production to take down Scampi. Michael Wilson has essentially turned into Jerry Rice in the past three games Marvin Harrison Jr doesn’t play. Harrison’s status for week 15 is up in the air and that will be a big factor for Gin. Pick: Madam Sack takes care of business.

Sweeney Deez and Zaukas (3) vs. Art of the Deal (6)

This is one of the more intriguing matchups of the first round and that’s reflected in the ticket prices. The average ticket price for this matchup is $345 compared to $177 across the other three playoff matchups. Some have hypothesized that people want to the future MVP Drake Maye, but the committee’s research suggests that people are dying to see Nine in person. Coming off his strongest game as a pro, Nine’s confidence is sky high and he feels like he can lead Art to the title. Despite being the higher seed, SDZ is actually a slight underdog here. X-factors: Tony Pollard and Darren Waller. After being dormant for much of the season, Pollard popped off for 28 points this week. That’s the type of explosiveness SDZ will need to make a playoff run. With Waller working his way back in the lineup, Art really needs him to revert to early season form. Pick: Art exacts revenge for last year and starts his playoff run.

Pigs on the 7th Rank (4) vs. The Jackson Brownes (5)

Longtime nemeses meet again. The injury to Zach Ertz has Pigs scrambling to find a suitable tight end replacement (probably Cade Otton). Meanwhile, JB is going to need some hero-level performances from Josh Allen and Bijan. Yahoo’s model favors JB, but the committee’s model favors Pigs. There’s potentially some solid arbitrage you can get on this betting line if you shop around to the different books. The main criticism of Pigs comes down to Patrick Mahomes. That Sunday night performance was among the grossest of his career, and it came at the absolute worst time for Pigs. X-factors: Nobody. Pick: Pigs on the 7th wins a shootout as Allen, Bijan, Gibbs and Brissett all go for a million.
