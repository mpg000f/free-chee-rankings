As we finish up the 4th year of the league, the committee thought it prudent to lookback and summarize the first four years of the league and grade everyone’s performance. We’ll be comparing each league owner to a US president. Given the accusations of bias from certain corners of the league, we devoted some time to developing a proprietary algorithm to properly rank these teams. Chat “Chuck G” GPT assisted with the math here. The algorithm is like the old BCS formula in that its on a scale of 0-1 and is sure to infuriate some people. Without further ado, let’s discuss the basics of said algorithm. Each team is assigned a Power Score that combines regular season performance, scoring strength, playoff resume and historical shame for finishing in last place. Each stat is normalized so the different scales are comparable and then the formula weights them as follows: • Win Percentage- 40% o Regular season only. We’re not giving double credit for playoff wins. • Points For- 35% • Point Differential- 5% o Strong defense deserves a little boost • Playoff Success- 30% o 3 points per championship o 2 points per finals appearance o 1 per semifinal appearance • Penalty for last place finishes All of the above is combined into a single Power Score, which determines the rankings. The results reward scholars. Please see the far right column. (Note: Zaukas and Boyle had a tie so there’s one less game for them)

Sweeney’s metrics are strong all the way around. He has three finals appearances in four years with two championships to show for it. After sneaking in as the last team in the field this year, he thrashed the juggernaut Scampi and rolled to the title. Some would call him the most clutch performer in the league given his win percentage is tied for 5th best and points for are 8th best. He knows how to peak at the right time. That’s the sign of a great coach. Comparison: FDR. Like Franklin Delano, Sweeney has been clutch when it matters most. While FDR weathered the Depression and World War Two, Sweeney has delivered clutch victories when facing elimination like his 5 straight wins to end the season this year. Neither of them compiled stats in peacetime, they both dominated in high stakes situations.
