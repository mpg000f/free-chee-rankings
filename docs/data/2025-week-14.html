Pigs on the 7th Rank
Deez
▲ 1

The league has been buzzing about the trade Pigs executed for over a week now and how could we not be given the performance that Jahmyr “On His Horse” Gibbs put together Sunday against the Giants. That trade catapulted Pigs from a decent playoff team to legitimate title threat and it was only possible because of his incredible foresight in acquiring Jacoby Brissett. Let’s revisit that transaction. It was September 4th, the opening day of the season and it was a stormy morning. The Zombie Room was buzzing at Bloomfield and rain pounded the windows. He scooped Brissett off waivers without knowing two months later it could lead to a franchise altering trade. That trade enabled Pigs to defeat Work Ass in a game with massive playoff implications this weekend. A loss would have Pigs in 7th place, firmly on the playoff bubble with the other 6-6 teams and only narrowly above Stewed C. The 49.9 point outburst from Gibbs allowed him to hold off Work Ass and move himself into 5th place. With one more win, Pigs will be a near lock for the playoffs. That output by Gibbs was good for the 14th-best game by any running back this century and the best this season, narrowly edging Jonathan Taylor’s 48.1 point output from a couple of weeks ago.

Predicted Finish: 9-5 and likely 4th place. He finishes with the two worst teams in the league in Gorlock and Senior Coke.
